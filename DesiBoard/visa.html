Visa Types and Details

Navigating visa requirements and processes can be complex. At Desi Board, we provide comprehensive support for various types of U.S. visas. Below, you'll find detailed information about different visa categories, their requirements, and links to official resources for further assistance.

HTB (H-1B) Visa

The H-1B visa is a non-immigrant visa that allows U.S. companies to employ foreign workers in specialty occupations. This visa is valid for up to three years and can be extended for a total of six years.

Key requirements for the H-1B visa include having a job offer from a U.S. employer, possessing specialized knowledge or a bachelor's degree or higher, and meeting the prevailing wage requirement.

For more details and application processes, visit the USCIS H-1B page.

L-1 Visa

The L-1 visa is designed for intra-company transferees who work in managerial positions or have specialized knowledge. It is available for employees of multinational companies who are being transferred to a U.S. office.

There are two types of L-1 visas: L-1A for managers and executives, and L-1B for employees with specialized knowledge. The L-1A visa is valid for up to seven years, while the L-1B visa is valid for up to five years.

For more information, visit the USCIS L-1 page.

B-1/B-2 Visa

The B-1 visa is for individuals traveling to the U.S. for business purposes, while the B-2 visa is for tourists. Both visas are typically issued for a duration of six months and may be extended.

Applicants must demonstrate that they intend to return to their home country after their stay and have sufficient funds to cover their expenses while in the U.S.

For additional details, visit the U.S. Department of State B-1/B-2 page.

F-1 Visa

The F-1 visa is for foreign students who wish to study in the U.S. at an accredited institution. This visa allows students to remain in the U.S. for the duration of their studies and can include optional practical training (OPT) for up to 12 months.

Key requirements include acceptance into a SEVP-certified institution, proof of sufficient financial resources, and intent to return to the home country after completing studies.

For more information, visit the SEVIS page and USCIS F-1 page.

STEM OPT

STEM OPT is an extension of the F-1 visa for students who have completed a degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics) field. This extension allows students to work in the U.S. for an additional 24 months beyond the initial 12 months of OPT.

Requirements include having a job directly related to the student's field of study and working for an employer enrolled in E-Verify.

For more details, visit the SEVIS STEM OPT page.
